How proficiency is judged. An operator qualifies on a method when every reference sample they test passes both ASTM precision checks for that method's parameters: repeatability r (their duplicate readings A and B agree within the method's repeatability) and reproducibility R (the mean of their readings agrees with the reference value within the method's reproducibility). Repeatability proves the operator is consistent; reproducibility proves they are accurate against an independent result — together they demonstrate the in-house measurement reproduces the third-party lab. Qualification is per method (D4052 gravity, D5191 RVP, D86 distillation) so an operator can be signed off on the instruments they've demonstrated. Each instrument’s daily verification gates only its own method: the Grabner pentane Ptot (16.19–16.53 psi) gates D5191 RVP; the Tanaka AD-7 and density-meter verifications gate D86 and D4052 respectively. A method whose verification is not passing is BLOCKED even if the numbers agree; a method whose verification is left “to be defined” still scores but is annotated on the record. (The D86 and D4052 verification procedures are still to be confirmed.) DVPE precision is level-dependent (larger allowance at higher vapor pressure); distillation precision is slope-dependent (steeper curve → larger allowance), computed per point from the recorded curve. All coefficients are editable and flagged pending confirmation against the current D4052 / D5191 / D86 editions; the D4052 SOP is still to be written. Records are the evidence package for replacing third-party rack-release testing at Fort Worth.
